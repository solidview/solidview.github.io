Financial data, information, news and analysis is being produced and distributed at an ever increasing pace which, is likely to accelerate further with the widespread use of large language models.

The author asserts that the average quality of existing research is low and, that most analysis is based on mistaken assumptions, biased incentives, incorrect economic theories and herding behaviour. The markets can subsequently to a large extent be considered inefficient, and the failure to realise and strategise upon these insights is likely a significant driver of low industry average active returns.

This site is a channel for the author to publish analysis with the goal of correcting the aforementioned issues. The analysis should culminate in transparent and actionable investment decisions.

About

The author of this site is an industry professional that is passionate about financial markets. However, a strong argument can stand on its own legs without resting on the credentials of the author.

The purpose of this website is not to gather views, to spread a message or to capitalise in any way shape or form on the potential readers. Rather it is to have a relatively transparent record of the thoughts and actions of the author. Publishing it will also act as a check against hindsight bias.
